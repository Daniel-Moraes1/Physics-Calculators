One Dimension Inelastic Collisions

In an inelastic collision, energy is lost in collision despite momentum remaining constant, which is different from a Perfectly Elastic Collision. The objects collide, but do not merge together as they would in a Perfectly Inelastic Collision.

Input five values of data into the table, and the last one will fill when you hit solve. The right box will display the change in energy before and after the collision.

Input Collision Data

Mass of Object 1
Velocity of Object 1
Mass of Object 2
Velocity of Object 2
Final Velocity Object 1
Final Velocity Object 2

Change in Energy
